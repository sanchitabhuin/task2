Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here is the timeline of Mr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.

  • October 15, 1931:Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Kalam graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in physics.
  • 1955: He joined the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) to pursue aerospace engineering.
  • 1960: Kalam completed his aerospace engineering degree and joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) of the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO).
  • 1969: He transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and played a significant role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III.
  • 1980: Kalam was appointed as the Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), where he spearheaded the development of ballistic missiles, including Agni and Prithvi.
  • 1982: He became the Scientific Advisor to the Indian Defense Minister and the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization.
  • 1988: Kalam played a pivotal role in India's nuclear weapons tests, codenamed Pokhran-II, which established India as a nuclear power.
  • July 25, 2002: Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. He became the first scientist and first bachelor to occupy the presidency.
  • July 25, 2007 After serving a term of five years, Kalam's presidency ended, and he handed over the reins to his successor, Pratibha Patil.
  • Post-Presidency: Kalam continued to be involved in various educational, scientific, and societal initiatives. He wrote several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which inspired and motivated people across the globe.
  • July 27, 2015: Abdul Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Meghalaya. His death was widely mourned, and he was given a state funeral with full military honors.

List of Award

  • 1981: Padma Bhushan (1981)
  • 1990: Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • 1997: The highest Indian civilian award Bharat Ratna (1997)
  • 2007: The Royal Society, UK has awarded Dr Kalam with the King Charles-II Medal for Science and Technology in October 2007.
  • 2008: He received the Woodrow Wilson Award in 2008
  • 2009:
    1. The Royal Academy of Engineering, London conferred on him the International Medal 2008.
    2. The Hoover Board of Awards presented him the Hoover Medal 2008 at New York in April 2009.
    3. The Aerospace Historical Society in Collaboration with the Graduate Aerospace Laboratories (GALCIT) at the California Institute of Technology awarded him the 2009 International Von Karman Wings Award in September 2009.

    • Abdul Kalam's contributions to science, missile technology, and his vision for a developed India made him one of the most beloved and respected figures in the country. His teachings and ideas continue to inspire millions even after his passing.
